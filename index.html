    TBI Statistics

    In Singapore, Traumatic brain injury (TBI) accounts for up to 75% of trauma-related deaths.

    In 2013, according to the National Trauma Registry, there were a total of 4,815 cases reported. For all levels of injury severity, the greatest proportion of the trauma patients was in the elderly (aged 65 years and above), and the majority of the hospitalised cases were due to falls.

    Community Programs

    As the care of patients with severe TBI requires heavy utilisation of resources. The healthcare burden of these patients extends beyond the acute critical care phase. Given the complexity and wide variety of programs observed, there is a lack of programs available to help with community reintegration.
